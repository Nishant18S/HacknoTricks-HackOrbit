‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between subsidized and unsubsidized loans? +
Subsidized loans don't accrue interest while you're in school, during grace periods, or deferment. Unsubsidized loans accrue interest from the day they're disbursed. Subsidized loans are need-based, while unsubsidized loans are available regardless of financial need.
How much can I borrow in student loans? +
Federal loan limits vary by year in school and dependency status. Undergraduates can borrow $5,500-$12,500 per year. Graduate students can borrow up to $20,500 per year. Private loan limits depend on your school's cost of attendance.
When do I start repaying my student loans? +
Federal loans have a 6-month grace period after graduation, leaving school, or dropping below half-time enrollment. Private loans vary by lender but typically have similar grace periods. Interest may accrue during the grace period.
Can I get student loans with bad credit? +
Federal student loans don't require credit checks for most borrowers. Private loans typically require good credit, but you may qualify with a creditworthy cosigner. Some lenders offer loans specifically for borrowers with limited credit history.
What happens if I can't repay my student loans? +
Federal loans offer deferment, forbearance, and income-driven repayment plans. You may also qualify for loan forgiveness programs. Contact your loan servicer immediately if you're having trouble making payments to discuss options and avoid default.
Are student loans tax deductible? +
You may deduct up to $2,500 per year in student loan interest on your federal tax return, subject to income limitations. This deduction is available even if you don't itemize deductions. Keep records of interest paid from your loan servicer.
